Lumpectomy

Lumpectomy is a breast-conserving surgery where only the tumor and a margin of normal breast tissue surrounding the lesion are removed. Depending on the size and location of the excision, reconstruction may be necessary to restore normal breast volume and contour.

Lumpectomy Reconstruction

A lumpectomy that is small and is located laterally or inferiorly can often be closed primarily without significant contour change to the breast.

A moderate size lumpectomy may be reconstructed using fat transfer or local tissue rearrangement.

A large lumpectomy often leaves the breast distorted. In patients who are candidates for breast reduction or breast lift (mastopexy), a defect that is located medially or laterally may be removed and the breast reshaped using an anchor-type incision breast reduction/mastopexy approach (Wise pattern). Other large defects may be corrected with an implant or a local flap. Additional fat transfer may be used to enhance breast contour and volume.
