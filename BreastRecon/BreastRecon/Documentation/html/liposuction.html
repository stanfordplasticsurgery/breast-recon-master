Liposuction
Typical Post-Operative Symptoms

  • You may experience temporary soreness, numbness, stinging, throbbing, burning sensation, redness, swelling, bruising and discomfort at the surgery sites. Most of these symptoms will subside in 6-8 weeks.

  • Bruising may be apparent beyond the areas of liposuction, partially due to gravity.

Activities

  • Get plenty of rest, but not bed rest. Start walking as soon as possible. This reduces the post-operative swelling and decreases the chance of blood clots. Signs of blood clot in your leg include swelling, warmth, and redness. Contact our office immediately if you develop these symptoms.

  • Activities involving the affected sites should be minimized for approximately 2 weeks.

  • Strenuous exercise and activities such as sports should be avoided for 3-4 weeks, depending on the extent of the surgery.

  • You can usually resume work and social activities in about 1 week.

  • Do not drive until you are no longer taking pain medications (narcotics).

Showering and Hygiene

  • You may shower over all your incisions after 48 hours. Pat all incisions dry with a towel after showering.

  • Do not take baths or go into the hot tubs or swimming pools until all the drains have been removed and all incisions and drain sites have completely healed.

Dressing & Incision Care

  • Remove any outer gauze dressing 48 hours after surgery. Do not remove the tape/steri strips or skin glue that are across the incisions.

  • The tape/steri strips or skin glue will begin to peel away after a few days. After 2 weeks you may remove any remaining tape/steri strips or skin glue.

  • All sutures are dissolvable and do not need to be removed.

  • Examine your incisions daily. It is normal to have some swelling, bruising, and discomfort around the incision site. It may take several days or weeks for these symptoms to resolve.

  • You can apply moisturizer or silicone sheets to your scars or start scar massaging starting 3 weeks after surgery when all the incisions have healed.

  • Avoid sun exposure to scars for at least 12 months after your last surgery. If sun exposure is unavoidable the always use sun block with 35 SPF or higher.

Undergarments

  • You will be discharged wearing a pressure garment over the treated area. Continue to wear a compression garment (i.e. spandex type clothing) in the area where the liposuction was performed for 24 hours per day (except when showering) for at least 2 weeks post-op. Most patients wear pressure garment for 6-8 weeks.

Diet & Lifestyle

  • Avoid cigarette smoking, second-hand smoking, and nicotine products including nicotine gum or patches for at least 4 weeks after surgery. Nicotine causes delayed wound healing and poor circulation.

  • Drink plenty of fluids (especially water), and eat a balanced diet. Avoid caffeine, soda, tea, and alcohol as they can cause dehydration.

  • Decreased activity and pain medications can promote constipation, so you may want to add more raw fruit and vegetables to your diet and take a stool softener as needed.

  • You may gradually resume normal activities after 1 week.

Medications

  • Take your antibiotic and pain medication as prescribed.

  • Resume all your home medications unless you are instructed otherwise by your doctor.

  • Do not take aspirin or any products containing aspirin during the 2 weeks after surgery, unless approved by your surgeon.

  • Do not drink alcohol when taking pain medications.

When to Call your Physician

  • Fever of 101.5F (38.3 C) or higher

  • Chest pain

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath

  • Excessive leg swelling

  • Excessive redness, swelling, bruising, bleeding, separation of the skin, drainage, or pus from the incision.

  • Severe or increased pain not relieved by medication

  • Persistent nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea
