Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)
Nipple Reconstruction Bandages
Your reconstructive plastic surgeon may use either antibiotic ointment, xeroform and a bulky dressing or a nipple guard instead of a bulky dressing to protect your nipple immediately following the procedure. You may remove these dressings after 24-48 hours and shower. Please follow the “Nipple Reconstruction” instructions under “After Surgery” tab.
Nipple Tattoo
You may decide to have your nipple and aerolar complex tattooed or just the aerola around the reconstructed nipple. This process usually takes place approximately 2-3 months post surgery. The breast tissue will need a chance to recover from surgery as swelling is common postoperatively. Allowing this time to heal not only helps with having accurate placement of the nipple/aerola but it will also allow the tattoo color to be better absorbed into the skin. Having a nipple tattoo is a painless process and takes approximately 1 hour in our outpatient office. Our nurse practitioner will tailor the color specifically to you. You may drive yourself to and from this appointment.
