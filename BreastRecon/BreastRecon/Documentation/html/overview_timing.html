Timing of Breast Reconstruction
When should I get my breast reconstruction?

Breast reconstruction can be done immediately following mastectomy or delayed after all cancer treatments are completed.

Who are good candidates for immediate reconstruction?

Immediate reconstruction is best in patients with early stage breast cancer.

The advantages to immediate reconstruction include:

  • The patient wakes up with a breast mound, and possibly a fully reconstructed breast.
  • The breast footprint and skin envelope are preserved so additional procedures to recreate the breast landmarks and to stretch the skin envelope are avoided.
  • The immediate reconstructed breast tends to look more natural.

The disadvantages to immediate reconstruction include:

  • It is not a good option for patients with advanced breast cancer where negative tumor margins may not be achieved during the initial cancer surgery, or if extensive adjuvant therapies are needed after surgery.
  • Post-operative radiation therapy can compromise the aesthetic results of the reconstructed breast. Revision surgeries may be required.
  • Cancer recurrence may require modification or removal of the reconstruction.
Who are good candidates for delayed reconstruction?

Delayed reconstruction is recommended for patients with advanced breast cancer or those who are undecided about having reconstruction at the time of cancer treatment. Patients with multiple comorbidities who are not able to tolerate a long surgery are also advised to delay their reconstruction.

The advantages to delayed reconstruction include:

  • It allows time to make sure all of the cancer is treated.
  • It gives the patient time to decide on a reconstructive option.

The disadvantages to delayed reconstruction include:

  • Absence of (a) breast(s) for a prolonged period of time.
  • Takes a longer time to complete the reconstructive process.
  • The breast landmarks have to be recreated and additional skin is usually recruited from a donor site for the reconstruction.
  • The delayed reconstructed breast tends to look less natural than an immediate reconstructed breast.
Are there other options for reconstruction?

If you have advanced disease but do not want delayed reconstruction, you can consider Staged reconstruction. This involves placing a temporary spacer (tissue expander) at the time of mastectomy to preserve the breast footprint and to keep the skin envelope out to stretch while the patient is completing her cancer treatment.

The advantages to staged reconstruction include:

  • Allows the patient to complete radiation treatment and all cancer treatments before undergoing definitive reconstruction with an implant or a flap.
  • Minimizes the loss of breast skin envelope or breast landmarks while waiting for definitive reconstructive surgery.

The disadvantages to staged reconstruction include:

  • Takes a longer time to complete the reconstructive process.
