What to Expect After Free Flap Breast Reconstruction
Post Op Day #0 - the day of surgery
  • Ice chips
  • Bed rest with head elevated >45 degrees and knees flexed >45 degrees
  • Foley (urinary catheter)
  • IV fluids
  • Blood draw post procedure
  • Warming blanket
  • Abdominal binder (for DIEP/TRAM/SIEA flaps)
  • PCA (pain pump) for pain control
  • Flap checks every 1 hour
Post Op Day #1
  • Clear liquid diet-no caffeine
  • Out of bed to chair with assistance
  • Foley is removed if patient is capable of getting out of bed reasonably
  • IV fluids
  • Blood draw for labs in am
  • Warming blanket while in bed
  • Abdominal binder (for DIEP/TRAM/SIEA flaps)
  • Surgical bra is placed (z-bra, mastectomy camisole-surgeon preference)
  • PCA (pain pump) is transitioned to oral pain medication
  • Flap checks every 1 hour
  • Incentive Spirometry x 10 per hour while awake
  • Drain teaching by nurse
Post Op Day #2
  • Regular diet-no caffeine
  • Out of bed to chair and ambulating-flexed at the waist forward to relieve tension on abdominal incision line
  • Foley discontinued if it hasn’t already been removed 
  • IV fluids are decreased to adjust for oral intake
  • Warming blanket discontinued
  • Abdominal binder (for DIEP/TRAM/SIEA flaps)
  • Surgical bra
  • Oral pain medication
  • Flap checks every 2 hours
  • Incentive Spirometry x 10 per hour while awake
  • Drain teaching by nurse
Post Op Day #3
  • Regular diet-no caffeine
  • Out of bed to chair and ambulating-flexed at waist forward
  • IV fluids are discontinued
  • Abdominal binder (for DIEP/TRAM/SIEA flaps)
  • Surgical bra
  • Oral pain medication
  • Flap checks every 4 hours
  • Incentive Spirometry x 10/hour while awake
  • Drain teaching by nurse
  • Possible discharge home if unilateral flap
Post Op Day #4
  • Regular diet-no caffeine
  • Out of bed to chair and ambulating-flexed at the waist forward
  • Abdominal binder (for DIEP/TRAM/SIEA flaps)
  • Surgical bra
  • Oral pain medication
  • Flap checks every 4 hours
  • Incentive Spirometry x 10/hour while awake
  • Drain teaching by nurse
  • Possible discharge home if bi-lateral flap
